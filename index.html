Bio

I'm a Graphics Software Engineer at Intel working on the oneAPI Rendering Toolkit. My focus is on XPU ray tracing, distributed rendering, and scientific visualization within OSPRay. Outside of Intel, I work on XPU ray tracing, GPU/WebGPU scientific visualization and rendering, and other projects involving massive scientific data visualization across computing platforms from HPC clusters to the web browser. I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science in 2021 at the Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute at the University of Utah, advised by Valerio Pascucci. Before joining the Ph.D. program at Utah I obtained a B.S. in physics with a minor in computer science at the University of California, Riverside in 2014. My research interests cover a range of areas in scientific visualization and computer graphics including: distributed rendering, virtual reality, in situ visualization and ray tracing.
